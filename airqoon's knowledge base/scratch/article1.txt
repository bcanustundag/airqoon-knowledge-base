What are we benchmarking?
The most common metrics are:

R2 (R-Squared)
It represents how well a
sensor performs in
comparison to the reference
instruments.
R2=1 means the sensor has perfect correlation.
R2=0 would mean no correlation to a reference
monitor.

U(exp) (Expanded Uncertainty)
When using U, we try to demonstrate that the
measurements uncertainty of the sensor system
is lower than some Data Quality Objectives
(DQO) previously stated.
For example, a device with U(exp) < 25% for
NO2 would meet the current European Directive
requirements for Indicative Measurements. This
is one of the conditions for Class 1 Certification
under future European specification..

Other metrics and factors
R2 and U have their limitations. Many other
metrics and statistics are applied:
RMSE, Slope of regression line, Intravariability,
Response time, Calibration, Hysteresis, Limit of
Detection, Repeatability, Short term drifts,
Long term drifts, Cross sensitivities,
Effect of temperature and humidity

CEN TC 264 WG 42

Future EU Technical Specification
A protocol describing specific
performance requirements and test
methods under prescribed laboratory
and field conditions for Gas and PMs.
Rules for a new Certification Scheme.
The categories are related to Data
Quality Objectives of methods set in the
Directive 2008/50/EC:
Class 1 sensor system: consistent
with indicative methods
Class 2 sensor system: consistent
with objective estimations methods
Class 3 sensor system: not formally
associated with any mandatory target
https://www.epa.gov/sites/production/files/201908/documents/gerboles_european_union_perspec
tive_on_sensor_standards.pdf

Airlab Challenge

Micro-sensors Challenge
The clarity provided by AIRLAB’s
transparent process enable buyers to
choose devices that best fit their
needs.
Over a period of four months, Airparif
tests the selected devices on 12
pollutants using no less than 28
parameters. Each sensor compete in one
or more category of usage (indoor air,
outdoor air, static device, mobile device,
public awareness…) and is evaluated
according to 5 criteria: Accuracy of
measurements, ergonomics, relevance of
the pollutants measured, portability and
cost. Special attention is given to data
accessibility. The shortlisted devices are
assigned a 1-5 star rating.

http://www.airlab.solutions/en/projects/
microsensor-challenge

MCERTs for PMs

IMCERTS Performance
Standards for Indicative
Ambient Particulate Monitors
The MCERTS certification scheme
was established by the UK's
Environment Agency and is built
around international and European
standards to ensure high-quality
environmental data monitoring.
The indicative dust monitoring analyser(s) can be
operated in one of two ways:
For qualitative measurements: Providing qualitative
measurement data for the analysis of particulate
pollution trends, and source identification studies based
for example on pollution roses etc. Such
application can rely on instrument factory calibration
only.
For quantitative measurements: Providing measurement
data with the uncertainty defined for
indicative instruments (±50%), as specified by the Air
Quality Directive (2008).

https://www.csagroupuk.org/services/m
certs/
