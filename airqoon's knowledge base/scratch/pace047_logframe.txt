Specific Objective (Outcome)
Students, teachers, and municipal staff in Defne district have improved capacity to monitor, understand, and respond to air quality risks in school environments, resulting in measurable improvements in protective behaviour and institutional environmental service capacity.
Outcome Indicator 1: Percentage of participating students who demonstrate improved knowledge of air quality health risks and adoption of at least two protective behaviours in post-survey, disaggregated by sex.
0% (no prior structured education on school air quality delivered; baseline established through pre-survey, May 2026)
At least 70% of participating students (disaggregated by sex: at least 70% girls and 70% boys) demonstrate improved knowledge and protective behaviour adoption by April 2027
Pre-post student surveys administered by project coordinator; gender-disaggregated analysis report; April 2027
Students remain enrolled throughout project period; survey participation rates adequate; school calendar disruptions do not prevent workshop delivery

Outcome Indicator 2: Percentage of trained mentor teachers who have integrated at least one air quality-related project activity into their regular teaching practice by project end, disaggregated by sex.
0% (no structured air quality capacity in place for school staff; baseline confirmed through pre-training needs assessment)
At least 80% of trained mentor teachers (disaggregated by sex) report active integration of air quality project activities into teaching practice by April 2027
Post-training follow-up survey; structured reflection session records; project coordinator field visits; April 2027
Mentor teachers remain in post throughout project period; school administrations support ongoing activity; no major curriculum restructuring

Outcome Indicator 3: Degree to which Defne Municipality Environment and Health units have operationalised air quality monitoring data into at least one service planning or communication activity by project end.
No systematic air quality monitoring data available to municipal units; no trained staff in this area (confirmed through partnership agreement)
Defne Municipality has produced at least one documented output (report, public communication, or service planning decision) using project air quality data by April 2027
Municipal staff training records; documented municipal output (report or communication); project coordinator verification; April 2027
Municipal staff trained remain in post; municipality commits to post-project system ownership; institutional priorities do not shift during project period
