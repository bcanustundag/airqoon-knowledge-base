# Analysis: Airqoon Core Use Cases

This document synthesizes Airqoon's primary commercial use cases based on market positioning, hardware capabilities (Unit L, Unit M, Unit N), and software platforms (Lens, Map). By clearly defining the problem and solution for each use case, Airqoon can target distinct customer personas more effectively.

---

## 1. Industrial Fenceline & Perimeter Monitoring
**Target Sectors:** Cement, Mining & Quarries, Oil & Gas, Iron & Steel, Power Generation, Chemical Plants.
**Key Hardware:** Unit L (Outdoor), Unit N (Noise)

**The Problem:** Traditional stack (chimney) monitoring systems do not capture fugitive dust or diffuse gas emissions that blow off-site. These unmonitored emissions often lead to community complaints, regulatory fines from bodies like the Çevre İl Müdürlüğü, and damaged public relations.
**The Airqoon Solution:** Deploying a mesh of solar-powered Unit L sensors around the facility's perimeter. When coupled with an anemometer, Airqoon Lens uses wind-rose correlations to identify exactly which facility activity caused a pollution spike.
**Outcomes:** 
- Shifts environmental management from reactive to proactive.
- Can reduce community complaints by up to 80%.
- Provides an affordable alternative to expensive reference stations ($100k+ vs $2.5k-$5.5k).

## 2. Municipal Air Quality & Noise Monitoring Networks
**Target Sectors:** Metropolitan & District Municipalities, Environmental Agencies, Smart City Programs.
**Key Hardware:** Unit L, Unit N

**The Problem:** Official reference stations are sparse. Local governments lack neighborhood-level data to address citizen complaints, map the Urban Heat Island effect, or prove the success of Clean Air Zones (CAZ) and green infrastructure.
**The Airqoon Solution:** A dense, city-wide network of Airqoon units deployed in under 5 minutes per site. The data feeds into the internal Airqoon Lens platform for policy-making, and the Airqoon Public Map for citizen transparency.
**Outcomes:** 
- Enables data-driven public health interventions.
- Increases citizen trust through the public map and complaint portal.
- Proven success in municipalities like Kadıköy, Bursa, Mudanya, and İnegöl.

## 3. Indoor Air Quality (IAQ) for Commercial & Public Spaces
**Target Sectors:** Shopping Malls, Schools & Universities, Hospitals, Corporate Offices, Hotels, Entertainment Centers.
**Key Hardware:** Unit M (Indoor)

**The Problem:** Poor indoor air quality (high CO2, VOCs, PM) degrades public health, reduces worker productivity, and creates liability. Facility managers lack continuous visibility into HVAC efficiency, which is also a growing requirement for ESG and sustainability reporting.
**The Airqoon Solution:** Compact Unit M sensors distributed across high-traffic indoor zones. These units track PM, CO2, VOCs, and T/RH, integrating with Building Management Systems (BMS) or operating standalone to provide real-time dashboards and alerts.
**Outcomes:** 
- Verifiable proof of healthy indoor environments (crucial post-COVID).
- Early detection of HVAC failures or inefficiencies.
- Automated data streams for ESG and sustainability reporting.

## 4. Organized Industrial Zone (OIZ) Environmental Management
**Target Sectors:** OIZ Management, Free Zones, Industrial Parks, Logistics Hubs.
**Key Hardware:** Unit L, Unit N

**The Problem:** When an industrial park spans hundreds of acres with dozens of tenants, identifying the specific tenant responsible for an emission event that triggers complaints is nearly impossible without localized data.
**The Airqoon Solution:** A mix of perimeter monitoring around the entire OIZ and internal corridor monitoring. The AI-backed Lens platform attributes sources to specific tenants, providing individual dashboards for each facility and a master dashboard for OIZ management.
**Outcomes:** 
- Evidence-based tenant management and accountability.
- Positions the OIZ as a "Green OIZ", increasing attractiveness for international investments and eco-friendly financing.

## 5. Research, Scientific Studies & Emergency Response
**Target Sectors:** Universities, Research Institutes, NGOs, Disaster Response Teams (e.g., Earthquake Recovery).
**Key Hardware:** Unit L

**The Problem:** Researchers and emergency responders often need rapid, reliable, and verifiable air quality data in remote or rapidly changing environments where reference-grade monitors are too fragile, bulky, or expensive to deploy.
**The Airqoon Solution:** Unit L's 5-minute deployment, 14-day solar battery autonomy, and NB-IoT/4G connectivity make it perfect for temporary or urgent campaigns. 
**Outcomes:** 
- Generates publishable, traceable data for scientific studies (e.g., PACE Hatay project).
- Provides immediate situational awareness in disaster zones (e.g., Kahramanmaraş earthquake dust monitoring).
- Builds long-term institutional relationships through pilot projects.

## Strategic Takeaway
Airqoon is highly versatile. To maximize sales effectiveness:
- **Lead with Pain Points:** Pitch solutions (e.g., "reduce community complaints", "prove HVAC efficiency") rather than technical features.
- **Differentiate from Stack Monitoring:** Clarify that Airqoon is an *ambient/immissions* monitoring solution designed for spatial intelligence, not a CEMS (Continuous Emission Monitoring System).
- **Leverage Regulations:** Utilize incoming indicative monitoring standards (CEN TS 17660) and ESG reporting mandates (EU Taxonomy) to drive urgency in adoption.
