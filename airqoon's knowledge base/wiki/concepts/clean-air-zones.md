# Clean Air Zones (CAZ)

> Designated urban areas where vehicles failing to meet emission standards are restricted or charged. A growing regulatory framework that creates demand for air quality monitoring infrastructure.

---

## Definition

A Clean Air Zone (CAZ) is a geographic area where local authorities restrict or charge vehicles that don't meet minimum emission standards. The goal is to reduce traffic-related air pollution and protect public health.

Related concepts:
- **LEZ** (Low Emission Zone) — common in Scotland
- **ULEZ** (Ultra Low Emission Zone) — London's expanded scheme
- **ZEZ** (Zero Emission Zone) — Oxford's pilot scheme

---

## Classification (UK Framework)

| Class | Vehicles Covered |
|-------|-----------------|
| **A** | Buses, taxis, private hire vehicles |
| **B** | Class A + heavy goods vehicles (HGVs) |
| **C** | Class B + light goods vehicles, minibuses, vans |
| **D** | Class C + cars, motorcycles (broadest scope) |

### Minimum Emission Standards

| Vehicle Type | Minimum Standard |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Buses, HGVs | Euro 6 |
| Cars, taxis, vans | Euro 6 (diesel), Euro 4 (petrol) |
| Motorcycles | Euro 3 |

## Revenue Use

CAZ charges fund: zero-emission buses, vehicle replacement subsidies, hydrogen development, cycling infrastructure, public transport improvements.
