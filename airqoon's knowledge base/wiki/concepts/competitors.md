# Competitive Landscape

> A high-level overview of the competitive environment for indicative air quality monitoring, focusing on direct competitors to Airqoon.

---

## The Market

The indicative air quality sensor market is growing rapidly as cities and industries seek hyper-local, cost-effective monitoring solutions to supplement traditional, expensive reference stations. The competitive battlefield is largely defined by **data accuracy (certifications)**, **platform capabilities**, **maintenance models**, and **pricing strategies**.

## Key Competitors

Airqoon's primary competitors in the hardware (Unit L/M) and software (Lens/Map) space include:

### 1. [[wiki/entities/oizom|Oizom]] (Polludrone)
- **Strengths**: Highly ruggedized (IP66, -20°C), wide connectivity options, ISO 17025 calibration, measures up to 9 gases + UV.
- **Weaknesses**: Heavy (8 kg), short battery backup (~24h), higher price point (>$8,000), complex maintenance, lack of public map features in their Envizom platform.

### 2. [[wiki/entities/kunak-technologies|Kunak Technologies]] (Kunak AIR Pro)
- **Strengths**: Certification leader. Aggressively pursues and markets compliance with international standards (CEN/TS 17660, MCERTS, EPA). Extremely transparent with co-location field study results showing high correlation (R² > 0.85) and low uncertainty.
- **Strategy**: Builds trust through robust, publicly available performance data. Targets professional and government networks requiring high-end supplementary data.

### 3. [[wiki/entities/bettair|Bettair]] (Bettair MK2)
- **Strengths**: Strong EU presence. Achieved Class 1 CEN/TS 17660-1:2021 accreditation. 
- **Strategy**: Innovative pricing and maintenance model. They bundle software subscriptions and EU connectivity into the upfront hardware and replacement cartridge costs ("Zero Maintenance Cost" / "Free Software" marketing). Uses pre-calibrated, interchangeable cartridges to eliminate complex on-site calibration.

---

## Competitive Vectors & Airqoon's Positioning

1. **Certifications (CEN/TS 17660, MCERTS)**: Competitors like Kunak and Bettair are leveraging Class 1 certifications as primary sales tools. *Airqoon must formalize and market its compliance to remain competitive.*
2. **Maintenance & Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)**: Bettair's cartridge replacement model simplifies OPEX calculations for buyers. *Airqoon's SIM replacement workflow needs to be positioned effectively against this.*
3. **Software Ecosystem**: Oizom's Envizom lacks advanced features like public maps, compare modes, and GenAI reports. *Airqoon Lens and Map are strong differentiators that need to be highlighted in sales conversations.*
