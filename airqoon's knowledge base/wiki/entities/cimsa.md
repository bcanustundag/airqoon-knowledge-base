## Relationship to Airqoon

Çimsa was previously listed as a pursued tender target in the [[wiki/entities/airqoon|Airqoon entity page]] (`EBRD/Çimsa Mersin tender pursued`). The relationship has now advanced to a **formal EBRD-mandated RFP** where Airqoon submitted a Technical Proposal (v3) in consortium with Istanbul University-Cerrahpaşa.

### ESAP Action 3.4
The EBRD requires Çimsa to implement a comprehensive **Dust Management Plan** for the Mersin plant area of influence (plant, quarries, access roads, Tekke/Yaka settlements) as a condition of financing. This includes deploying "smart and robust dust monitoring systems" to establish credible baseline data before the 5th CAC kiln becomes operational.

### Airqoon's Proposed Scope
- 12-month consulting assignment (4 work packages)
- 6-node PM sensor network deployment using Airqoon hardware
- [[wiki/entities/airqoon-lens|Airqoon Lens]] platform instance configured and handed over as a permanent operational asset
- ML-based source attribution model fusing satellite, ground sensor, meteorological, and process data
- AERMOD/HYSPLIT dispersion and trajectory modelling
- Full Dust Management Plan delivery with ESAP compliance

## Dust Categories at Mersin Plant

1. **Raw Material Dust** — crushing, grinding, handling of limestone/clay/correctives (coarse particles)
2. **Kiln & Clinker Process Dust** — calcination, kiln firing, clinker cooling (fine, reactive — CaO, SiO₂, trace heavy metals)
3. **Cement Dust** — clinker grinding, milling, storage, packing (very fine, respiratory hazard)

## Strategic Significance

- **First EBRD project** for Airqoon — positions the company as a credible environmental consultant for international financial institutions
- **Template deal** — replicable for other EBRD/IFC cement and heavy industry engagements (Oyak Çimento, Çimentaş, etc.)
- **Lens lock-in** — contractual platform handover creates long-term SaaS relationship
- **Prior art** — Çimsa has an existing dedusting programme for its Eskişehir plant (previous EBRD deal), making Mersin the second engagement
