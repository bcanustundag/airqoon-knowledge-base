# An Introduction to International Standarization, Testing & Certifications

## Source Metadata
- **File**: `articles/1a5a8ef68ae3edcafb87c70f9bad47b7e4e1e48bf5a07bb3cb524df1fe445839.pdf`
- **Author/Org**: Kunak Technologies (Miguel Escribano)
- **Date**: April 2021 (v2.0)

## Summary
A presentation by competitor Kunak Technologies outlining the market trends for Non-regulatory Supplemental and Informational Monitoring (NSIM) systems and the evolving landscape of sensor certifications and testing protocols. It serves as a marketing tool that positions Kunak as a knowledgeable leader in sensor standardization.

## Key Takeaways

### Market Trends
1. **Hybrid Networks**: Cities seeking hyper-local monitoring combining reference and sensor-based systems.
2. **Beyond Regulatory**: Developing countries adopting cost-effective solutions for new regulations (UNEP initiatives).
3. **COVID-19 Catalyst**: Increased focus on reducing air pollution levels for health and sustainability.

### Testing & Certification Programs Listed
- **US EPA Air Sensor Toolbox**: Protocols for PM2.5 and Ozone.
- **CEN TC 264 WG 42**: Future EU tech specs for gas and PM, defining Class 1, 2, and 3 based on Data Quality Objectives (DQO).
- **AQ-SPEC by AQMD**: Public evaluations under ambient and controlled conditions (not a formal certification).
- **ASTM WK64899**: Standardized tests and assessment criteria.
- **Wildland Fire Challenge**: EPA evaluation of multi-pollutant sensor systems during fires (Kunak was awarded).
- **INERIS/LNE Certification**: Voluntary certification (Divisions A, B, C) aligning with EU Directives.
- **VITO Validation Center**: Independent performance determination under various conditions.
- **Airlab Challenge**: Micro-sensors challenge by Airparif (accuracy, ergonomics, portability, cost).
- **MCERTS for PMs**: UK certification scheme based on indicative (±50% uncertainty) measurements.

### Performance Metrics Highlighted
- **R² (R-Squared)**: Correlation with reference monitors.
- **U(exp) (Expanded Uncertainty)**: Needs to be <25% for indicative measurements of NO2 under EU Directive.

### Kunak's Positioning
- Kunak uses this comprehensive overview to market the **Kunak AIR Pro**, highlighting its "type approval" design, scalability, and previous awards (e.g., Wildland Fire Challenge, AQ-SPEC best option).

## Relevance to Airqoon
- Provides a comprehensive list of certification targets Airqoon should be aware of or pursue (especially CEN/TS 17660 and MCERTS).
- Shows how competitors leverage their knowledge of complex standards as a sales and marketing tool to build trust.
