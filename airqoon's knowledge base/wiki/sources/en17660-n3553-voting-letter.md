# CEN/TC 264 N3553 — Voting Result on Preliminary New Work Items prEN 17660-1 & prEN 17660-2

## What This Document Is

A formal letter (dated **2026-03-11**) from the Secretariat of CEN/TC 264 "Air quality" (Nicole Perschau, VDI/DIN, Düsseldorf) to all CEN/TC 264 members, announcing the **successful adoption** of two preliminary new work item proposals from **Working Group 42 (WG 42)**:

- **prEN 17660-1** — *Air quality – Performance evaluation of air quality sensor systems – Part 1: Gaseous pollutants in ambient air* (conversion of CEN/TS 17660-1:2021)
- **prEN 17660-2** — *Air quality – Performance evaluation of air quality sensor systems – Part 2: Particulate matter in ambient air* (conversion of CEN/TS 17660-2:2024)

The decisions were registered as **Decisions 1486 and 1487**.

## What This Means

Both documents are being upgraded from **Technical Specifications (CEN/TS)** to full **European Standards (EN)**. This is a major regulatory step — once finalised as ENs, compliance with these standards may become mandatory under EU air quality directives and purchasing/procurement frameworks.

### Voting Results

**prEN 17660-1:**
- National Members approving: **13 / 0 disapproving** → **100% approval**
- Blue-Type (EEA) Members: **12 / 0** → **100%**
- Abstentions (not counted): 11 countries including Czech Republic, Denmark, Greece, Ireland, Lithuania, Malta, Netherlands, Norway, Spain, Switzerland, **Türkiye (TSE)**
- Votes not cast: 10 countries (Croatia, Cyprus, Estonia, Iceland, Latvia, Luxembourg, North Macedonia, Romania, Serbia, Slovakia)

**prEN 17660-2:** Identical voting pattern — 13 approving, 0 disapproving, 100% approval.

### Notable Comment

**France (AFNOR)** submitted the only substantive comment (on prEN 17660-1):
> "The link with the new Directive 2024/2881 on air quality should be more explicit (rather than referring to the previous Directive 2008/50/EC, which will be repealed when the work is completed)."

This flags an important transition: the current TS documents reference **Directive 2008/50/EC**, but the newer **Directive 2024/2881** (the revised AAQD — Ambient Air Quality Directive) will supersede it. The EN versions will need to be aligned with the new Directive.

## Significance for Airqoon

- **TSE (Turkey) abstained** — not blocking but not championing the standard. Airqoon should monitor this, as TSE adoption into TS standards will affect Turkish regulatory requirements.
- The **EN conversion** (from TS to EN) signals that EN 17660 is maturing into a **mainstream certification pathway** for sensor systems in Europe. Airqoon's existing CEN/TS 17660 test results (referenced in its testing documentation) will carry forward but may need updating once the ENs are published.
- The mention of **Directive 2024/2881** is significant: this is the new stricter EU air quality directive with tighter PM2.5 and NO2 limits, setting the regulatory backdrop for near-future certification requirements.
- Airqoon's Unit test process document (`Airqoon-Sensor-Üniteleri-Test-Süreci-17660.md`) is directly built on EN 17660; this N3553 letter is the authoritative signal that the standard is being hardened into a full EN.
