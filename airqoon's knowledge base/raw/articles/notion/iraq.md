# Iraq

Iraq is currently one of the countries facing the most serious air pollution issues in the world. The country's complex history, geographical location, and economic structure bring together numerous factors that affect air quality.

## Causes of Air Pollution:

- Debris and waste left from military operations during the 2003 U.S. invasion
- Major conflicts against the Islamic State group between 2014 and 2017
- Vehicle emissions
- Use of generators due to weak electricity infrastructure
- Fires from oil and gas refineries
- Frequent dust storms (3-4 times a month)
## Effects:

- Increasing cancer cases (approximately 2,000 new cases annually in Basra)
- High cancer rates, especially among children
- According to the IQAir 2022 report, Iraq is one of the seven worst countries exceeding WHO guidelines
- Ranked 2nd globally in emissions from gas flaring, according to the World Bank
- Loss of 25,000 hectares of arable land annually (due to desertification)
- Some regions of the country are expected to become uninhabitable in the next few decades.

## Monitoring Efforts:

- Currently, there are 29 air quality monitoring stations in the National Air Quality Monitoring Network.
- A joint Air Quality Network project initiated by the MEED Foundation in partnership with IQAir (May 8, 2024).
- A network of **28 sensors** has been established in Sulaymaniyah, Halabja, and Kirkuk.
- The sensors measure PM1, PM2.5, PM10, and the Air Quality Index every 15 seconds.

## Recommendation

  **The monitoring network could be expanded in Basra city and the Rumaila oil region.**

  Air pollution in Iraq can be attributed not only to the country itself but also to Western oil companies. Oil giant BP is flaring at the **Rumaila** oil field near Basra. Higher cancer rates have been observed, many of which affect children. The situation has worsened to the point where Iraq’s Environment Minister, Jassem al-Falahi, was forced to acknowledge a link between the two, though the Oil Minister continues to deny any connection between air pollution and cancer rates.
