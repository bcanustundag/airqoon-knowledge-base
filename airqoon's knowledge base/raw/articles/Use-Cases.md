### Perimeter monitoring to assess the environmental impact of industrial facilities

Given the air quality, most of the time, the focus is only on the local area. But the reality is that air quality is a global issue that affects everyone. Although many countries have improved their air quality over the past 40 years, higher levels of air pollution have been seen, especially in rapidly industrializing areas. For livable and sustainable cities, industrial facilities must manage their environmental contribution.



One of the environmental management responsibilities of industrial facilities is to keep environmental pollution under management. Ecological resources are utilized in all production processes, and production residues, mainly gases, are emitted into the environment.  Manufacturers must continuously improve their production processes to maximize quality while minimizing costs and environmental footprint. Protecting workers and surrounding communities from air pollution and dust is essential to healing.



The industry mainly leads to air pollution due to the use of fossil fuels as an energy source, the release of waste gases into the air without being filtered, and the wrong choice of location. Complexes with large-scale industrial activities are constant sources of various environmental pollutants, such as fine dust, sulfur dioxide (SO2), nitrogen dioxide (NO2), carbon monoxide (CO), ozone (O3), volatile organic compounds (VOC), polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAH) and heavy metals. Policies to reduce air pollution offer a win-win strategy for both climate and health, reducing the burden of disease attributable to air pollution and mitigating near- and long-term climate change.


Air quality monitoring aims to detect and control on and off-site fugitive emissions of air pollutants. An air monitoring system is used to reduce the potential exposure of surrounding communities and employees and to comply with the recommended guidelines.  By continuously monitoring the environment, manufacturers can manage air quality and its contribution to the environment and detect leaks.


Airqoon's monitoring system consists of two components: sensor units mounted around an industrial facility and a cloud platform that aggregates the data collected by these sensors and translates it into useful information.



### Public information and protection - Raise public awareness

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), data, 7 million people die every year due to diseases caused by air pollution, and 9 out of every 10 people breathe polluted air. Air pollution is accepted as the most significant environmental health threat and ranks 6th among the world's causes of death, outdoor pollution, and 8th place indoor pollution. Air quality monitoring is a vital part of preventing health problems. Data from the air monitoring system can be used to improve exposure forecasts and raise public awareness about air pollution.



Among the most dangerous air pollutants are particle pollution concentrations smaller than 2.5 micrometers, also known as PM2.5. PM2.5 has been declared a carcinogenic substance by the WHO. These fine particles can settle deep in the lungs and enter the bloodstream, causing health problems and premature death. However, we can prevent premature deaths due to air pollution.  Continuous air pollution monitoring is necessary to make measurements regularly and to take the necessary measures.


In many places worldwide today, with increasing awareness of air pollution, people are forming communities to know what air they are exposed to. Lawsuits against governments for failing to protect public health due to air pollution show that public awareness is growing. One of the most critical factors is the air sensor systems, which are increasingly attracting attention. With the proliferation of sensors and cloud technology, people's access to data is becoming easier, and as informed societies grow, their expectations of public authorities increase.


Thanks to its compact size, sensor networks that allow placing them intensively as part of the urban infrastructure can provide high-resolution spatial and temporal data on the monitored pollutants. Cost-effective air monitoring systems, which encourage innovation in air monitoring approaches, help reduce public concerns while at the same time promoting behavior change to reduce pollution and are a good tool for the adoption of preventive habits.


Airqoon's mission is to make air quality data more accessible everywhere. The Airqoon sensor units enable the reference network to increase and expand while providing more spatial and temporal data.

### Urban air pollution monitoring networks - Expand spatial coverage

Air pollution is one of the biggest threats to people's health and a problem that is getting worse. Air quality monitoring networks are inadequate; thus, there is insufficient data on where and when air pollution is worse. This lack of information makes it difficult for cities to take action to improve their state of the environment. With air quality monitoring, cities allow them to assess pollution levels, identify sources of pollution and local hotspots, raise public awareness, monitor policy implementation and effectiveness and measure progress.



Air sensors are becoming a popular alternative to government monitoring tools, and they can potentially increase the monitoring data pool. Overall, these alternative sensors could help map pollution levels in the city; they can help reach urban areas that are not accessible using standard monitors and complement an expanding network of regulatory classes in any city.



The development of air sensors allows the air quality monitoring network to increase and expand while providing more spatial and temporal data opportunities to communities. At the same time,  sensors offer an affordable alternative to reference stations. With low-cost air sensors, it is possible to eliminate blind spots in the city by maximizing monitoring coverage without exceeding the project's defined budget.


Expanding the measurement network by integrating a supplementary system with the existing monitoring network ensures an accurate assessment of urban air quality. Numerous studies have shown that air quality varies significantly by suburban and street. Air quality can be improved by setting up a monitoring network in high-traffic areas to know when vehicle emissions reach dangerous levels and take action accordingly. With an air monitoring network installed around industrial facilities, the environmental impact of plant emissions can be measured.


Using low-cost sensors that are easy to use and maintain, Airqoon helps to its stakeholders with targeted installations to monitor environmental air pollution and provide new data and new information on urban basins. Urban air quality monitoring and mapping is an essential foundation for urban planning.

### Heat island effect measurement

Heat islands are urbanized areas with higher temperatures than the outer regions. Urban areas, where structures are highly concentrated and greenery is limited, become islands with higher temperatures than in the external areas. Heat islands contribute to higher levels of air pollution. Due to the heat island effect, people living in cities are at greater risk than those living in suburban or rural areas. Urban heat islands cause many adverse effects, such as health problems, increased energy consumed for cooling, air pollution, water scarcity, and climate change.

Climate change and the urban heat island effect are co-evolving factors that can amplify future heat effects. Extreme weather events such as heat waves, droughts, and climate change are becoming more common, causing health and water supply problems. Increased exposure to extreme heat from climate change and the urban heat island effect threatens fast-growing urban settlements worldwide. Increasing urbanization will also result in megacities where phenomena such as urban heat islands can be exacerbated.



Urban heat islands can, directly and indirectly, affect health. High temperatures can affect the health of city dwellers, leading to widespread discomfort, respiratory problems, sunstroke, dehydration, fatigue, and even increased mortality due to heat stroke.  Vulnerable populations are particularly at risk during these events. Increasing urban heat is expected to reduce workforce productivity and outdoor work capacity.


Heat islands increase the demand for air conditioning, namely electricity, to cool buildings. This demand for increases is a financial burden for many predominantly low-income households. Companies that provide electricity use fossil fuel plants to cover much of this price, increasing air pollutants and greenhouse gas emissions. At the same time, high temperatures can directly increase the rate of ozone formation at ground level.


Increasing public investment to green infrastructure is a critical way to solve heat islands. The Airqoon air monitoring system facilitates complex data collection on particulate matter, gaseous pollutants and meteorological factors. Offering accurate real-time data, the Airqoon air monitoring system allows citizens sensitive to air pollution and temperature to plan their day and limit their exposure by sharing it with communities through local authorities.

### Assessing the impact of investments on green tech

Technology has the potential to impact the environment positively. Making green investments to reduce pollution is a new initiative for many industries. However, green technology is an essential tool in reducing carbon emissions, air pollution, and overall energy use, which can play a more active role in helping to improve the environment. Especially in the cement, mining, and oil and gas industries with a high contribution to environmental pollution, it is essential to pay attention to the environmental impact of investments, as air quality is directly affected in settlements near production facilities.



Green technology uses technology and science to create environmentally friendly products and services. This technology helps prevent climate damage in the future and mitigate the damage already done by reducing or eliminating its resources. The main goal of green technology is to minimize the negative impact of new technologies on the planet by using clean energy. As the tech market grows and consumers become more environmentally conscious, clean tech products allow them to reduce their carbon footprint and resource use. Governments worldwide are beginning to invest in green technology businesses to decarbonize the economy and make their countries more energy efficient.


Although they seem like two very different issues, climate change, and air pollution are closely linked. They both share familiar sources of emissions. Particulate matter and gas emissions contribute to both climate change and air pollution. Interventions to reduce them simultaneously can be cost-effective and optimize outcomes, producing better results than addressing them separately. Most hazardous air pollutants include gaseous pollutants from the burning of fossil fuels. The application of green technological innovation is helping businesses adopt cleaner and renewable energy in the production process and reduce the use of fossil fuels. It is also possible to measure the impact of investments made in this direction by monitoring the change in air quality.


The Airqoon solution accurately assesses pollution levels in manufacturing facilities, providing real-time, data-driven, actionable environmental intelligence.  It enables the impact of the investments to be measured and to understand how much change it can create.  Facilities can manage their ecological contribution by measuring and monitoring air quality more broadly in their area of responsibility.
